    Do you love teaching but fed up with all the paperwork and planning?

    Do you want a better life-work balance?

    Primary teachers in Scotland currently spend an average of 72 hours each term on planning!

    We have designed an online tool to reduce the time you spend planning and allow you to spend more time on the things that really matter to you.

    What our final product will contain:
    • Yearly class timetable
    • Termly Planning of all subject areas
    • Download pdf of plans
    • Es and Os/benchmarks from termly planning transferred to weekly plans
    • Lesson plan templates
    • IDL topic planning facility - ability to share ideas
    Ideas for the future:
    • Tracking and assessment
    • Communicate lessons covered to parents weekly via social media
    • Reminders of upcoming dates/holidays
    • School noticeboard - communciation
    • And much more - based on your feedback & suggestions

    Imagination is the limit!

    Hello!

    Who are we?

    We are a husband & wife team looking to solve the very important problem of excessive planning in primary school teaching.

    We are the perfect team for solving the problem of excessive planning! Yvonne is an experienced primary teacher, and Andy is a highly experienced software engineer. Together we have built a tool for teachers to help them achieve a better life-work balance

    Yvonne Boyle

    Primary Teacher

    Experience in Perth and Kinross, Stirling & Clackmannanshire Councils

    Andy Boyle

    Senior Software Engineer / Consultant

    Experience mainly in the financial sector with JP Morgan, Barclays & HSBC

    Follow @@timetoteachzone @*facebook*@ @*youtube*@

    Why are we doing this?

    “As an experienced primary teacher with a young family of my own, I found juggling teaching and family life very difficult.

    My husband, who worked as a senior software engineer for a large bank, couldn’t believe the things I would spend my evenings and weekends doing. In my 10 years of teaching I only taught at the same stage 2 years running so I was constantly learning a new stage and “re-inventing the wheel”.

    When I moved to a new local authority with different forms, plans and ways of working I struggled to keep up with the changing demands. This was when we came up with the idea of Time to Teach.

    Andy knew that it was entirely feasible to create software which would automate planning for teachers, however we knew it was a big project. He has now been building the software since September 2017 and we are on schedule to have it ready by August 2018.“
    Yvonne

    Help us on our journey by proving that this is a tool which teachers in Scotland deserve to have. We know most teachers already pay for essentials like glue sticks, pencils and paper, so we will always aim to keep costs to teachers as low as possible. Our ultimate goal is to secure deals at Local Authority and/or School level so that teachers don’t have to pay.

    Teachers are professionals, and they deserve to be given the best tools to allow them to do their job to the best of their abilities. Our tool will give teachers more Time to “Teach“
